# `<conclusion>{:dn}`


`<conclusion>{:dn}` is a block container for the concluding content
of a section. It groups content without adding a heading, numbering,
or any other visible chrome, and is typically placed at the end of
a [`<section>{:dn}`](section) (or similar sectional component) to
mark the closing prose distinct from the body.


<AttrPropDisplay name='conclusion'/>


---

### Example: `<conclusion>{:dn}` at the end of a section


```doenet-editor-horiz
<section>
  <title>Linear equations</title>

  <subsection>
    <title>Examples</title>
    <p><lorem generateSentences="2"/></p>
  </subsection>

  <conclusion>
    <p>In the next section we will turn to quadratic equations.</p>
  </conclusion>
</section>
```



The `<conclusion>{:dn}` itself adds no visible formatting; it simply
groups the concluding content so that surrounding components (and
authoring tools) can locate it.



---

### Example: `<conclusion>{:dn}` after a list of `<task>{:dn}` items


```doenet-editor-horiz
<problem>
  <title>Lab procedure</title>
  <introduction>
    <p>Complete the following tasks in order.</p>
  </introduction>
  <task>Gather the equipment.</task>
  <task>Calibrate the instrument.</task>
  <task>Record the measurement.</task>
  <conclusion>
    <p>Submit your recorded measurements.</p>
  </conclusion>
</problem>
```



Like [`<introduction>{:dn}`](introduction), `<conclusion>{:dn}` does
not participate in the list-item numbering of the [`<part>{:dn}`](part)
or [`<task>{:dn}`](task) items inside the surrounding section.
