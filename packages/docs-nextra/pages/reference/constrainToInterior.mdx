# `<constrainToInterior>{:dn}`

`<constrainToInterior>{:dn}` is a [Graphical](../document_structure/essentialConcepts#component-types)
component that constrains a `<point>{:dn}` to lie inside a referenced graphical region (such as a
`<polygon>{:dn}`, a `<circle>{:dn}`, or a `<rectangle>{:dn}`). When the point is dragged outside the
region, it is moved to the nearest point on the region's boundary.

<AttrPropDisplay name='constrainToInterior'/>


---

### Example: Constrain a `<point>{:dn}` to the interior of a `<polygon>{:dn}`

```doenet-editor-horiz
<graph size="small">
  <shortDescription>A triangle and a point constrained to its interior</shortDescription>
  <polygon name="tri" vertices="(3, 5) (-4, -1) (5, 2)" filled />
  <point name="P" styleNumber="2" coords="(7, 8)">
    <constrainToInterior>$tri</constrainToInterior>
  </point>
</graph>
```

The point starts outside the triangle and is immediately pulled to the nearest boundary point. Drag
it: it is free to move anywhere inside the triangle, and is held against the boundary when pushed out.


---

### Example: Constrain a `<point>{:dn}` to the interior of a `<circle>{:dn}`

```doenet-editor-horiz
<graph size="small">
  <shortDescription>A circle and a point constrained to its interior</shortDescription>
  <circle name="disk" center="(0, 0)" radius="3" filled />
  <point name="P" styleNumber="2" coords="(5, 2)">
    <constrainToInterior>$disk</constrainToInterior>
  </point>
</graph>
```

The point cannot leave the disk. Released outside the circle, it snaps to the nearest point on the
circumference.


---

### Example: Constrain a `<point>{:dn}` to the interior of a `<rectangle>{:dn}`

```doenet-editor-horiz
<graph size="small" xmin="-6" xmax="6" ymin="-6" ymax="6">
  <shortDescription>A rectangle and a point constrained to its interior</shortDescription>
  <rectangle name="box" vertices="(-3, -2) (3, 2)" filled />
  <point name="P" styleNumber="2" coords="(0, 0)">
    <constrainToInterior>$box</constrainToInterior>
  </point>
</graph>

<p>P is at $P.coords.</p>
```

Any rectangle works as a "drag boundary" for a point.


---

### Attribute Example: relativeToGraphScales

```doenet-editor-horiz
<graph size="small" xmin="-10" xmax="10" ymin="-1" ymax="1">
  <shortDescription>A wide-aspect graph with a circle and a point constrained to its interior using graph scales</shortDescription>
  <circle name="disk" center="(0, 0)" radius="1" filled />
  <point name="P" styleNumber="2" coords="(5, 0.2)">
    <constrainToInterior relativeToGraphScales>$disk</constrainToInterior>
  </point>
</graph>
```

When the graph's $x$ and $y$ axes use very different scales, `relativeToGraphScales` measures distance
to the boundary in pixel-like units rather than mathematical units, so the "nearest" point matches what
the user sees. Compare to omitting the attribute, where the nearest-point calculation uses raw
mathematical distance.
