# ` <p>{:dn}`




` <p>{:dn}` renders a paragraph of text.


## Attributes and Properties 
 <AttrDisplay name='p'/> 
 <PropDisplay name='p'/>

---

### Example: Two paragraphs


```doenet-editor-horiz
<p>Chocolate cake sugar plum chocolate bar brownie chupa chups chocolate tart.</p>
<p>Shortbread chocolate marshmallow macaroon candy canes. Shortbread jujubes chocolate apple pie wafer croissant.</p>
```



All text enclosed within an opening and closing ` <p>{:dn}` forms a paragraph.



---

### Example: ` <p>{:dn}` within sectional components


```doenet-editor-horiz
<section>
<p>Chocolate cake sugar plum chocolate bar brownie chupa chups chocolate tart.</p>
</section>      

---

<p>Shortbread chocolate marshmallow macaroon candy canes.</p>


<exercise>
<p>Bear claw pastry liquorice jelly beans shortbread sesame snaps.</p>
</exercise> 
<problem>
<p>Dessert oat cake marzipan bear claw wafer ice cream ice cream.</p>
</problem> 
```



Paragraphs can be nested within sectional components such as a ` <section>{:dn}`, ` <example>{:dn}`, ` <exercise>{:dn}`, or ` <problem>{:dn}`.



---

### Example: ` <p>{:dn}` within an ` <li>{:dn}`


```doenet-editor-horiz
<p><alert>Love Potion:</alert></p>
<ol>
    <li><p>pickled turnips</p></li>
    <li><p>eye of toad</p></li>
    <li><p>powdered birchtree root</p></li>
  </ol>
```



The ` <p>{:dn}` component can be nested inside list items to provide additional line spacing.



---

### Example: Separate text and math content


```doenet-editor-horiz

  <function name="f">$userFunction</function>
  <point name="intercept">(0,$$f(0))</point>
  <line equation="y = $userFunction" name="userLine"/>

<p>Write the equation for a line in slope-intercept form:</p> 
<p><m>y = </m> <mathInput name="userFunction"/></p>
<graph grid="1 1" size="small">
    $intercept
    $f
</graph>
<p>What is the <m>y</m>-intercept of your line?</p>
  <answer>$$f(0)</answer>
<p>The slope of your line is:</p> 
  <answer>$userLine.slope</answer>
```



Frequently a ` <p>{:dn}` tag is used to separate lines of text and sections of math content, such as a ` <graph>{:dn}`, a ` <mathInput/>{:dn}`, or an ` <answer>{:dn}`. Try deleting all of the ` <p>{:dn}` tags from the example above to observe the effect.
