# `<equilibriumCurve>{:dn}`


`<equilibriumCurve>{:dn}` extends [`<curve>{:dn}`](curve) to mark a curve of equilibria of a dynamical system.
It is rendered solid when the equilibria are stable and dashed when they are unstable. Use it to display a
nullcline that consists of equilibrium points along a curve (for example in a bifurcation diagram or in a phase
plane) while making the stability visually apparent. The component accepts the full set of `<curve>{:dn}`
attributes, including spline `through` points and `flipFunction` for swapping the role of the variables.

<AttrPropDisplay name='equilibriumCurve'/>

---

### Example: Stable and unstable equilibrium curves


```doenet-editor-horiz
<graph xmin="-6" xmax="6" ymin="-6" ymax="6">
  <shortDescription>A solid spline of stable equilibria on the right and a dashed spline of unstable equilibria on the left</shortDescription>
  <equilibriumCurve through="(1, 2) (3, 4) (2, 6)"><label>stable</label></equilibriumCurve>
  <equilibriumCurve stable="false" through="(-1, 2) (-3, 4) (-2, 6)"><label>unstable</label></equilibriumCurve>
</graph>
```



Two spline curves of equilibria; the right curve is drawn solid (stable) and the left is dashed (unstable).
The `stable` attribute defaults to `true`; setting it to `false` switches the rendering to dashed.



---

### Attribute Example: switchable


```doenet-editor-horiz
<graph xmin="-6" xmax="6" ymin="-6" ymax="6">
  <shortDescription>A clickable equilibrium curve whose stability the learner can toggle</shortDescription>
  <equilibriumCurve name="C" switchable through="(-4, -3) (0, 0) (4, 3)" />
</graph>
<p>Click the curve to toggle. <c>C.stable</c> = $C.stable</p>
```



When `switchable` is set, clicking the curve toggles its `stable` state and the solid/dashed rendering. This
supports interactive classification of nullclines in phase-plane exercises.



---

### Example: Sketching a saddle-node bifurcation diagram


```doenet-editor-horiz
<graph xmin="-3" xmax="3" ymin="-3" ymax="3">
  <shortDescription>A saddle-node bifurcation diagram with a stable upper branch and an unstable lower branch</shortDescription>
  <xLabel>r</xLabel>
  <yLabel>x</yLabel>
  <equilibriumCurve through="(0, 0) (1, 1) (2, 1.4)" />
  <equilibriumCurve stable="false" through="(0, 0) (1, -1) (2, -1.4)" />
</graph>
```



A pair of `<equilibriumCurve>{:dn}` components sketches the upper (stable) and lower (unstable) branches of a
saddle-node bifurcation diagram for $\dot x = r - x^2$.
